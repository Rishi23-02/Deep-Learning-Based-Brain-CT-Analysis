CARE Foundation

CARE Foundation has been promoted by a group of medical scientists and technocrats with the stated mission of making advanced comprehensive healthcare affordable and accessible to all. Founded in 1997 with its head office in Hyderabad, it is a non-profit organization registered under the Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Society Registration Act. The Foundation is run by a board of eminent medical professionals and scientists dedicated to making tertiary healthcare available to the general masses of India.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your brain MRI with S-SCAN.

Our AI achieves 98.4% diagnostic accuracy on a validation dataset of 500,000+ brain MRI scans, covering gliomas, meningiomas, white matter disease, and vascular malformations. Every AI result is independently reviewed by a board-certified neuroradiologist before the final report is issued.

A typical brain MRI session takes 30-45 minutes. Our AI processes scan data within minutes of acquisition. The final radiologist-verified report is delivered within 24 hours for standard requests, and within 4-6 hours for urgent clinical cases.

MRI is a non-invasive, radiation-free imaging technique using magnetic fields and radio waves — one of the safest diagnostic methods available. There are no known side effects. Patients with certain metal implants should inform their physician before scheduling.

S-SCAN supports native FHIR R4 and HL7 v2.x interfaces, enabling direct integration with Epic, Cerner, Meditech, and Allscripts. Our team provides full onboarding support and typical integration takes 5-10 business days.

All patient data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). S-SCAN is fully HIPAA and GDPR compliant with optional on-premise deployment. We conduct quarterly third-party security audits and maintain a full access audit log.

S-SCAN detects a wide range of neurological conditions: brain tumors (primary and metastatic), stroke and ischemic lesions, multiple sclerosis plaques, cerebral aneurysms, hydrocephalus, Alzheimer's biomarkers, traumatic brain injury, and white matter abnormalities.
